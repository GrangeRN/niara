📐 Psychometric Threshold Reference — How NIARA Categorizes Items

NIARA applies established psychometric criteria from classical test theory to classify each exam item. These thresholds are derived from the educational measurement literature including McDonald (1999), Haladyna & Rodriguez (2013), and NCSBN item development standards. All calculations are performed locally in your browser; no student data is transmitted.

🚨 Action Required — Before Releasing Grades
⚠ AWARD ADDITIONAL CREDIT A distractor was selected at high rates by high-performing students, suggesting a second answer may be defensibly correct due to wording ambiguity. SME review required before posting grades. Consider awarding credit for both the keyed answer and the flagged distractor for this administration.
🔑 VERIFY ANSWER KEY Stats suggest the keyed answer may be incorrect (negative point-biserial with high-performers avoiding it), OR for Fill-in-the-Blank: multiple students entered the same response and received zero credit — possible key typo, missing alternate answer, unaccepted rounding variant, or blank configured incorrectly in Brightspace. Verify key setup before posting grades.
📋 Item Revision Plan — Before You Use This Exam Again
✓ KEEPItem is performing within acceptable psychometric parameters. Statistics support retention without modification for the next administration.
✏ REVISEItem shows measurable psychometric weakness. Revision of the stem, answer choices, or key is recommended before the next administration. Not urgent — no grade action needed now.
🗑 RETIREItem is too psychometrically flawed to revise effectively. Remove from the item bank before the next administration. Document removal in the Faculty Action Log for accreditation records.

📉 Item Difficulty (p-value)
p < 0.40REVISEFewer than 40% correct — possibly too difficult or flawed
0.40 ≤ p < 0.50REVISE (borderline)Moderately difficult — verify appropriateness
0.50 ≤ p ≤ 0.95AcceptableIdeal range for nursing licensure-style items
p > 0.95REVISE (near-ceiling)Little contribution to score variance
The difficulty index (p) is the proportion of students who answered correctly. For SATA, difficulty is calculated as the proportion earning full credit on the item.
🔀 Discrimination Index (D)
D < 0.00VERIFY ANSWER KEYNegative — lower performers outscored upper group
D ≥ 0.00AcceptableUpper 27% mean minus lower 27% mean
The discrimination index compares item performance between the top and bottom 27% of scorers. Note for SATA/MAT: Not used as a flag trigger for these item types.

☑ Select All That Apply (SATA / M-S) — Special Rules

SATA items require all correct options to be selected for full credit. Negative point-biserial and negative discrimination index are not used as flag triggers for SATA because these statistics behave differently when multiple correct answers exist. The only valid pre-grade concern for SATA is when a confirmed-incorrect answer choice is selected at high rates.

Incorrect option selected ≥ 25% of studentsAWARD ADDITIONAL CREDIT considerationMay indicate that option is also defensibly correct
Correct answers for SATA are identified as options selected by more than 50% of students who earned full credit on the item. All other options are classified as distractors for analysis purposes.
🔗 Matching (MAT) — Special Rules

Matching items are analyzed at the prompt level rather than the item level. Double-keying does not apply to matching questions. The meaningful signal is when students consistently choose the same wrong match for a specific prompt, indicating conceptual confusion between two terms.

≥ 25% of students matched Prompt A → Response B (wrong)AWARD ADDITIONAL CREDIT considerationMay indicate wording confusion between two concepts
When the confusion is bidirectional (students mix up A→B and B→A symmetrically), it is reported as a single note. Recommendation language specifies the exact prompt pair and suggests rewording to provide greater conceptual clarity if needed.
✏ Fill-in-the-Blank (FIB) — Special Rules

FIB items are grouped by unique question stem, not by question title. Question titles in Brightspace are category labels (e.g., "Grams to Milligrams") that may apply to multiple different question stems in a randomized pool. Each unique stem is analyzed independently with its own statistics.

Correct answer clearly dominantKEEPKey functioning correctly
Item difficulty or PB concerningREVISEReview wording or acceptable answer list for next use
≥ 25% wrote same answer, got zero creditVERIFY ANSWER KEYPossible key typo, missing alternate, or rounding variant not accepted
Common FIB key issues include: the correct answer not entered in Brightspace, a spelling error in the key, a valid rounding variant not added as an accepted answer (e.g., "0.13" not accepted when key only has "0.125"), or the blank field configured incorrectly. Always verify the Brightspace answer key setup before posting grades when this flag appears.
📊 Longitudinal Confidence Tiers

For pool-based exams, individual questions may only reach 5–8 students per administration. Flags become more reliable as cumulative responses accumulate across semesters. Pre-grade flags (Verify Answer Key, Award Additional Credit) fire regardless of sample size.

Cumulative n < 10Insufficient DataNo post-exam revision flags generated — needs more administrations
Cumulative n 10–24Low ConfidenceFlags shown as preliminary signals only
Cumulative n 25–49Moderate ConfidenceFlags are meaningful — monitor trends
Cumulative n ≥ 50High ConfidenceStatistically defensible for accreditation documentation
Use the Longitudinal Report to track cumulative n and confidence tiers across cohorts. A question flagged REVISE at Low Confidence in one semester may resolve to KEEP at High Confidence after three administrations — or confirm the weakness. Longitudinal data separates cohort performance from item quality.

All thresholds reflect standards commonly applied in nursing education psychometrics and are intended to guide, not replace, faculty professional judgment. Items flagged by NIARA should be reviewed in context of course content, student population, and curricular goals before final decisions are made. For pool-based exams, single-semester statistics on items with fewer than 15 responses should be treated as preliminary signals only. Longitudinal data across multiple cohorts provides the most defensible basis for item revision or retirement decisions.
